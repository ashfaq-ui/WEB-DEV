REUSE
RECYCLE
REPAIR
DESCRIPTION Minimize waste generation by using fewer resources and avoiding unnecessary consumption. This involves practices like buying only what is needed, choosing products with minimal packaging, and opting for durable, long-lasting items. Extend the life of products by using them multiple times or finding new uses for them. This can include repurposing items, donating or selling goods, and opting for reusable products instead of single-use items. Convert waste materials into new products through processing. This helps reduce the need for raw materials and lowers environmental impact. Effective recycling involves separating waste correctly and supporting recycling programs. Extract energy or value from waste that cannot be recycled, often through processes like composting, waste-to-energy (incineration), or material recovery. This step is often considered a last resort before disposal.
GOAL Minimize waste generation at the source. Extend the life cycle of products and materials. Convert waste into reusable material to reduce the need for raw resources. Extract energy or other benefits from waste that cannot be reduced, reused, or recycled.
EXAMPLES
  • Bringing reusable bags when shopping.

  • Choosing digital documents over printed ones.

  • Implementing lean manufacturing techniques in industries.
  • Reusing glass bottles as decorative vases.

  • Donating clothes and electronics.

  • Refillable packaging initiatives by companies.
  • Recycling plastic bottles, cans, and paper products.

  • Using recycled materials in manufacturing processes.

  • Creating new products from recycled materials, like eco-friendly clothing made from recycled fabrics.
  • Generating biogas from organic waste.

  • Using incineration with energy recovery to generate electricity.

  • Composting food scraps and yard waste for gardening.
HOW TO IMPLEMENT
  • Consumer Habits: Buy only what you need, choose products with less packaging, and opt for quality over quantity.

  • Design & Production: Develop products that use fewer resources, have longer lifespans, and create minimal waste.

  • Energy & Water Conservation: Use energy-efficient appliances, reduce water waste, and streamline production processes.
  • Repair & Maintenance: Fix broken items instead of discarding them.

  • Repurposing: Find new uses for items (e.g., using glass jars as storage containers).

  • Sharing & Donation: Participate in second-hand markets, donate items, or set up sharing programs (e.g., community tool libraries).
  • Sorting Waste: Properly segregate recyclables like paper, plastics, metals, and glass.

  • Support Recycling Programs: Participate in municipal or community recycling initiatives.

  • Choose Recyclable Products: Opt for products made from recycled materials or that can be recycled at the end of their life cycle.
  • Energy Recovery: Incinerate waste to produce energy (waste-to-energy plants).

  • Composting: Turn organic waste into nutrient-rich compost for agriculture.

  • Material Recovery: Extract valuable materials from waste streams, such as metals from e-waste.
BENEFITS
  • Decreases the volume of waste generated.

  • Conserves natural resources and reduces pollution.
  • Reduces demand for new products.

  • Saves energy and resources involved in manufacturing.
  • Reduces landfill waste.

  • Lowers greenhouse gas emissions by conserving energy.
  • Reduces the volume of waste sent to landfills.

  • Produces renewable energy and valuable by-products.
HOW IT HELPS TO CLIMATE ACTION
  • Lowers Carbon Footprint:By consuming fewer products, we reduce the energy and emissions involved in manufacturing, transportation, and waste disposal.

  • Conserves Resources:Reducing demand for raw materials helps protect natural ecosystems and biodiversity.

  • Minimizes Pollution:Decreases air, water, and soil pollution by reducing waste generation and resource extraction.
  • Extends Product Lifecycles:Reduces the need for new products, saving resources and reducing waste.

  • Prevents Waste:Diverts items from landfills, where they could produce methane, a potent greenhouse gas.

  • Supports Circular Economy: Encourages systems where products are continually repurposed rather than discarded.
  • Reduces Emissions:Recycling materials like aluminum, paper, and plastic uses less energy than producing them from raw materials, leading to lower carbon emissions.

  • Decreases Landfill Waste: Prevents waste from decomposing in landfills and releasing methane.

  • Promotes Resource Efficiency: Turns waste into valuable raw materials, reducing the demand for natural resource extraction.
  • Energy Generation:Converts waste into energy through processes like incineration with energy recovery or biogas production, providing a renewable energy source.

  • Reduces Landfill Impact:Decreases the volume of waste that ends up in landfills, reducing methane emissions.

  • Enhances Soil Health:Composting organic waste creates nutrient-rich soil, which can improve carbon sequestration in soils.
